FOOD WASTAGE

Food wastage is a serious issue with environmental, economic, and social consequences. In India, 74 million tonnes of food—about 22% of the country's foodgrain output—is wasted annually. This is alarming, considering millions of people still go hungry every day.

Causes of food wastage

Food wastage is caused by improper storage, over-purchasing, inefficient distribution, lack of awareness, and excessive leftovers from households and events.

some of the causes of the food wastage are :

  1. Post-harvest losses: Poor storage, transportation, and handling lead to significant food loss.
  2. Lack of awareness: Many people waste food without realizing its impact.
  3. Social gatherings: Weddings and large events often result in excessive food wastage.
  4. Household waste: India ranks 111 out of 125 countries in the Global Hunger Index, yet 78 million meals are wasted annually in Indian households.

Consequences

Food wastage leads to hunger, environmental damage, financial loss, and depletion of natural resources.

The main consequences are :

  1. Hunger & malnutrition: Wasted food could feed millions of people.
  2. Environmental impact: Food waste contributes to climate change and resource depletion.
  3. Economic loss: India loses ₹50,000 crores worth of food every year

Solutions

Reducing food wastage requires better storage, mindful consumption, food donations, composting, and raising awareness.

The solutions are :

  1. Better storage & transportation: Investing in infrastructure to reduce post-harvest losses.
  2. Public awareness: Educating people on responsible consumption.
  3. Food redistribution: Donating excess food to those in need.
  4. Government policies: Strengthening regulations to minimize food waste

...Food is precious, and reducing wastage is a step toward a more sustainable future...
